May it be in the city or at the beach, your skin needs to be protected against the sunrays' damages. Scroll down and learn more!

The sun protection factor may increase, but it only indicates the protection against UVB rays!

A good sunscreen must also protect against UVA, which represent 95% of UV rays.

UVA rays are present all year long. Even on a cloudy day, even in the shade and they even pass through glass.

AVOID SUN EXPOSURE BETWEEN 11AM AND 4PM, when the sun's rays are the strongest.

UVB RAYS remain on the surface of your skin, causing it to tan and burn.

SHORT UVA RAYS penetrate deeper, and therefore cause more long-term effects, like premature aging and the development of skin cancers.

LONG UVA RAYS (represent 75% of total UV rays) penetrate skin even deeper. They cause serious damage, both in terms of skin's color and structure (wrinkles, sagging).

On a daily basis, if you work outside, apply sunscreen every two hours.

It only takes one hour in the sun for unprotected skin to sustain permanent damages.

WHEN MOVING AROUND, don't forget that some parts of your body are particularly vulnerable.

The face, neck and ears are common sites for skin cancer.

UVA rays can be reflected by many surfaces, like water, sand or concrete, making it important to protect yourself at all times.

A pair of sunglasses, a hat and an umbrella are essential for being well protected WHILE VACATIONING AT THE BEACH.

DON'T FORGET TO RE-APPLY YOUR SUNSCREEN after swimming, towel-drying or heavy perspiration.

20 minutes before sun exposure, apply the equivalent of a golf ball for your body and a teaspoon for your face.
